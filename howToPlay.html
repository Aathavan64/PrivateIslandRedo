HOW TO PLAY THE GAME

Download the 1st casefile

Once you’ve signed up as a freelance detective - registered with the Phester Rock Police Department (PRPD) - it’s time to hit the virtual pavement and start solving some cases. The Chief of Police for the PRPD hands out assignments. He will get you the files you need to get started and stay in touch with you throughout the investigation for updates on your progress.

Review the files - look for leads

Every good detective worth their salt is going to have a talent for sniffing out clues, but it also comes with experience and practice. In a casefile, the answer isn’t always obvious. You’ll have to use your training, research skills, and your powers of deduction to catch the criminal. Make sure you read your training packages thoroughly - they are full of helpful hints and tips that will allow you to hack into websites, analyse forensic clues, and pull together the facts.

Assemble clues - target a suspect

In any given case file there may be multiple suspects. In order to cross-reference clues, times of day, and alibi’s for people you need to take detailed notes. This can help you catch someone in a lie, or build a picture with missing pieces you know you need to look for. Don’t worry - the PRPD has set up their detectives for success. Make sure to use the facts chart and suspect list we’ve included in your case file download package.

Request a warrant - win the game!

You’ve done your research, questioned suspects and witnesses, and collected as much evidence as you can find. All the clues point to one prime suspect - and it’s time to take the criminal down. First, we need a judge to issue a warrant for their arrest. Head over to the Warrants Request page on the PRPD website and fill out the request form using the clues and info you’ve assembled. Be careful though. If you don’t have enough evidence, or you’ve implicated the wrong person, you’ll be in all kinds of trouble.
